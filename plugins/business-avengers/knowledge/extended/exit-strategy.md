## 1. Indie SaaS Acquisition Multiples

### Revenue Multiple Benchmarks (Acquire.com / Quiet Light 2024–2025)

| ARR Range | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| < $10K MRR | 2–3× ARR | Micro — mostly direct/word of mouth deals |
| $10K–$50K MRR | 3–5× ARR | Searchable market; most Acquire.com deals |
| $50K–$200K MRR | 4–6× ARR | Institutional Micro-PE interest begins |
| $200K–$500K MRR | 5–7× ARR | Multiple bidders; broker recommended |
| > $500K MRR | 6–10× ARR | Strategic acquirer territory |

**Multiple drivers (what increases your multiple):**
| Factor | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| NRR > 110% | +0.5–1× | Expansion revenue signals strong PMF |
| Churn < 2%/mo | +0.5–1× | Low churn = predictable ARR |
| Growth rate > 20% YoY | +1–2× | Growing products command premium |
| Owner hours < 10/week | +0.5–1× | "Automated" products = higher multiple |
| Revenue diversification | +0.5× | No single customer > 20% of ARR |
| Documentation quality | +0.25× | Buyers pay for knowledge transfer ease |
| Tech debt low | +0.25× | Auditable, clean codebase |

**Multiple killers (what decreases your multiple):**
- Single customer > 30% revenue: -1× or deal-breaker
- Founder-dependent (Bus Test < 5/10): -0.5–1×
- Revenue declining: -1–2× or unsellable
- Undocumented spaghetti code: -0.25–0.5×
- No payment processor history (< 12 months): -0.5×

## 2. Exit Types and Selection Framework

### Exit Type Comparison

| Exit Type | Best for | Timeline | Typical outcome |
|-----------|----------|----------|-----------------|
| Strategic acquisition | Product with strong user base or technology | 6–18 months | 5–10× revenue premium |
| Financial buyer (Micro-PE) | Profitable, systematic SaaS | 3–6 months | 3–5× ARR |
| Acqui-hire | Strong team, even if product struggling | 3–12 months | $500K–$2M per engineer |
| Marketplace sale (Acquire.com) | < $5M ARR | 1–4 months | Market rate multiple |
| FIRE / Self-sustaining | Passive income, no sale | Ongoing | Monthly cash flow |

### FIRE Plan (Financial Independence, Retire Early)

**Formula:**
```
FIRE Number = Annual expenses × 25 (4% withdrawal rule)

Example:
Annual expenses = $80K
FIRE Number = $80K × 25 = $2M

MRR needed (at 70% net margin, taking all as income):
$80K / 12 / 0.70 = ~$9,500 MRR
```

**SaaS FIRE milestones:**
| MRR | Annual Net (70% margin) | Life status |
|-----|------------------------|-------------|
| $3K | $25K | Side income |
| $10K | $84K | Ramen profitable |
| $15K | $126K | US median salary |
| $25K | $210K | Comfortable FIRE |
| $50K | $420K | Wealthy FIRE |

## 3. Acquisition Readiness Checklist

### Financial Due Diligence (3-Year Requirement)
- [ ] 3 years (or full history) of P&L by month
- [ ] Revenue reconciled to payment processor (Stripe export matches books)
- [ ] Churn cohort data available by month
- [ ] CAC by channel for last 12 months
- [ ] LTV calculation documented
- [ ] One-time vs. recurring revenue clearly separated
- [ ] All expenses documented (no personal expenses mixed in)

### Technical Due Diligence
- [ ] Codebase on GitHub/GitLab with commit history
- [ ] README with deployment instructions
- [ ] Environment variables documented (no hardcoded secrets)
- [ ] All third-party service credentials transferable (or can be replaced)
- [ ] Domain(s) and DNS transferable
- [ ] SSL certs, payment processor account transferable
- [ ] No IP disputes or open-source license violations

### Operational Due Diligence
- [ ] SOP documentation for all key processes
- [ ] Customer contracts (if any): transferable clauses
- [ ] No pending legal disputes
- [ ] Privacy Policy and Terms of Service current and GDPR/CCPA compliant
- [ ] Employee/contractor agreements documented

### Traffic & Marketing Due Diligence
- [ ] Google Analytics / analytics data access
- [ ] SEO traffic sources documented (no black-hat history)
- [ ] Email list ownership and data export capability
- [ ] Social media account transfer possible

## 4. Scale vs. Sell Decision Framework

### Scale Signal Checklist (reasons to keep and scale)
- NRR > 110% — expansion revenue shows product-market depth
- CAC Payback < 6 months — efficient growth machine
- TAM > $1B addressable — room to run
- Strong defensible moat (network effect, data, switching costs)
- Founder passion and energy intact
- Strategic value to potential acquirer going up over time

### Sell Signal Checklist (reasons to exit now)
- Market timing: strategic acquirers actively buying in your category
- Founder burnout — business can't grow without founder energy
- Better use of capital elsewhere: 10× opportunity vs. 2× risk
- NRR < 100% — slow decline is hard to reverse
- Tech debt so severe that rebuilding would take 12+ months
- Offer is > 5× ARR during growth phase (rare, take it seriously)

## 5. Scale Strategy Frameworks

### Ansoff Matrix — Growth Direction

| | Existing Product | New Product |
|--|-----------------|-------------|
| **Existing Market** | Market Penetration (safest) | Product Development |
| **New Market** | Market Development | Diversification (riskiest) |

**Recommendation:** Start with Market Penetration (go deeper in beachhead) before Market Development.

### Pricing Power Test
Can you raise prices 20% and retain 80%+ of customers?
- Yes → You have pricing power → raise prices, increase LTV
- No → Either no switching costs or commodity product → fix differentiation first

### Partnership / Distribution Scale Lever
Best distribution multipliers for SaaS:
1. **Integration marketplace** (Zapier, Slack App Directory) → PLG loop
2. **Agency/reseller channel** → 30% revenue share to agency for each customer
3. **API / embed** → Let others distribute your product for free
4. **White label** → Sell to B2B who sells to their customers

## 6. Exit Strategy Quality Standards (Phase 12)

**Scale/Exit Plan Checklist:**
- [ ] Current valuation estimate: MRR × appropriate multiple (benchmarked)
- [ ] Acquisition readiness score: due diligence checklist % complete
- [ ] Bus Test score stated
- [ ] FIRE number calculated and distance stated
- [ ] Scale vs. Sell decision made with explicit reasoning
- [ ] If scaling: growth lever identified (Ansoff matrix quadrant)
- [ ] If exiting: exit type selected with timeline
- [ ] Multiple improvement roadmap: top 3 factors to increase multiple

```markdown

**Scale/Exit Quality Check**
- Depth: [1–3] — [multiples benchmarked, specific exit type chosen]
- Evidence: [1–3] — [Quiet Light / Acquire.com comparables cited]
- Specificity: [1–3] — [FIRE number calculated, acquisition checklist scored]
- Acquisition readiness: [X% of checklist complete]
- Bus Test: [X/10]
- Estimated multiple: [X× ARR based on factors]
```
